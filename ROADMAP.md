# Jainverse -- Vision & Proposed Learning Journey

I am building a website called Jainverse with the goal of introducing Jain philosophy to modern readers, especially complete beginners, in a simple, structured and intellectually honest way.

The website is not intended to be a digital encyclopedia of Jainism.

Instead, it is intended to be a guided learning journey.

A beginner should be able to start from zero knowledge and gradually develop a coherent understanding of Jain philosophy without being overwhelmed by terminology.

## Core Principles

- Understanding before terminology
- Questions before definitions
- Insight before memorization
- Philosophy before ritual
- Progressive learning from beginner to advanced

Every concept should be introduced only after the reader understands the question that concept answers.

For example:

Instead of starting with "What is Jiva?", the journey begins with:
- What am I?
- Am I merely a body?
- What is consciousness?

Only then is the term Jiva introduced.

Similarly, readers should understand:
- Why karma enters before Asrava
- Why karma binds before Bandha
- Stopping new karma before Samvara
- Removing old karma before Nirjara

This allows terminology to emerge naturally from understanding rather than memorization.

The entire website will be available in:

- Marathi
- Hindi
- English

The goal is not merely translation but conceptually equivalent explanations across all three languages.
